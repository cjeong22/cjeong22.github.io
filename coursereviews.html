  • Difficulty: 6
  • Organization: 7
  • Usefulness: 8
  • How much did I like it: 9
  • Coursework: 9 Programming Projects, 1 Midterm, 1 Final
Kind of an anomaly of a course, which makes me enjoy it so much more.
CSCI 1260 dives into the intersection of formal methods, assembly/systems
programming, software development, and type theory. The professor is
brilliant, a very effective lecturer, and a kind person.
CSCI 0330 Fall 2023 w/ Thomas Doeppner
  • Difficulty: 8
  • Organization: 8
  • Usefulness: 9
  • How much did I like it: 9
  • Coursework: 7 Programming Projects, 9 Labs
So far (Fall 2023) my favorite class in the CS department. The
professor is a true pioneer of his field and extremely knowledgeable,
yet he is humble and cares deeply about his students. The course is
somewhat difficult; I would say the time commitment is similar to
that of CSCI 0150 but the work is much more intellectually
stimulating. Programming assignments are difficult and require
close attention to detail.

CSCI 0200 Spring 2023 w/ Kathi Fisler & Nick DeMarinis
  • Difficulty: 5
  • Organization: 5
  • Usefulness: 10
  • How much did I like it: 8
  • Coursework: 6 Programming Projects, 10 Labs, 1 Midterm, 1 Final
Everyone considering CS should take this class. Extremely applicable
content. I don't want to jump the gun here but I feel like this is
the most important class offered by the CS department here. Of
course, it is a bit of a logistical and organizational nightmare
due to the sheer size of the course, but that does not stop this
course from getting my highest recommendation. Programming assignments
are difficult at first but quickly become trivial. Midterm and Final
are very manageable.

I found this to be a pretty fun introduction to CS. Covering Java and
Object-Oriented programming practices, CSCI 0150 is a slow-paced yet
informative course on how to program. The work is not intellectually
stimulating but is vast in quantity.
